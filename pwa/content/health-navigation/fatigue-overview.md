# Fatigue Overview: Understanding Energy Patterns

## What Is Fatigue?

Fatigue refers to a sense of tiredness, lack of energy, or reduced capacity for activity. It's a common experience that can have many contributing factors.

## Common Contributing Factors

### Sleep

- Quality and quantity of sleep
- Sleep schedule consistency
- Sleep environment factors
- Sleep disorders

### Nutrition

- Adequate hydration
- Balanced nutrition
- Meal timing
- Nutrient considerations

### Activity Patterns

- Physical activity levels
- Rest and recovery balance
- Sedentary periods
- Exercise recovery

### Stress and Emotional Factors

- Life stressors
- Emotional well-being
- Work-life balance
- Support systems

## When to Discuss with a Clinician

Consider discussing fatigue with your care team if:
- It's persistent or worsening
- It significantly impacts daily activities
- It's accompanied by other concerning symptoms
- It's new or unusual for you

## Tracking Fatigue

Helpful information to track:
- When it occurs (time of day, after activities)
- Duration and severity
- What helps or worsens it
- Associated symptoms or patterns

## Important Note

This information is for health navigation and education only. It is not a diagnosis or medical advice. Persistent or severe fatigue should be evaluated by a licensed clinician.

## Next Steps

- Review your sleep, nutrition, and activity patterns
- Track fatigue patterns over time
- Share your observations with your care team
- For urgent concerns, seek immediate medical attention
