# Stress Physical: How Stress Affects Your Body

## Overview

Stress can manifest in physical ways that may surprise you. Understanding the physical effects of stress can help you recognize patterns and work with your care team on management strategies.

## Physical Manifestations of Stress

### Common Physical Symptoms

- Muscle tension and pain
- Headaches or migraines
- Digestive changes
- Sleep disruption
- Fatigue or low energy
- Changes in appetite

### Less Obvious Effects

- Skin reactions
- Immune system changes
- Hormonal fluctuations
- Cardiovascular responses
- Respiratory changes

## The Stress Response

### Fight or Flight

- Body's natural response to perceived threats
- Designed for short-term activation
- Chronic activation can cause problems
- Individual responses vary

### Recovery and Balance

- Rest and recovery periods are essential
- Stress management supports physical health
- Balance between activation and rest
- Long-term patterns matter

## Managing Physical Stress Effects

### Awareness

- Notice physical patterns
- Connect physical symptoms to stress
- Track what helps or worsens symptoms
- Share observations with care team

### Support Strategies

- Stress reduction techniques
- Physical activity and movement
- Adequate rest and recovery
- Support systems and resources

## Important Considerations

- Physical symptoms can have many causes
- Stress is one contributing factor
- Work with your care team for evaluation
- Individual needs vary significantly

## Important Note

This information is for health navigation and education only. It is not a diagnosis or medical advice. Persistent physical symptoms should be evaluated by a licensed clinician.

## Next Steps

- Track physical symptoms and patterns
- Note connections to stress or life events
- Discuss patterns with your care team
- Explore stress management strategies
