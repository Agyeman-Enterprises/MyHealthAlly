# Overtraining: Recognizing When More Isn't Better

## Overview

While regular physical activity is beneficial, too much training without adequate recovery can lead to overtraining. Understanding the signs can help you maintain a healthy, sustainable movement practice.

## What Is Overtraining?

### Definition

- Excessive training without adequate recovery
- Imbalance between stress and recovery
- Can affect physical and mental well-being
- Individual thresholds vary
- Requires attention and adjustment

### Contributing Factors

- Too much volume or intensity
- Insufficient rest and recovery
- Poor sleep quality
- High stress levels
- Inadequate nutrition
- Rapid increases in training

## Signs of Overtraining

### Physical Signs

- Persistent fatigue
- Decreased performance
- Increased injury risk
- Sleep disruption
- Changes in appetite
- Persistent muscle soreness

### Mental and Emotional Signs

- Mood changes
- Irritability or apathy
- Loss of motivation
- Difficulty concentrating
- Increased perceived effort
- Burnout feelings

## Prevention and Recovery

### Balanced Approach

- Adequate rest and recovery
- Gradual progression
- Listen to your body
- Support recovery with nutrition and sleep
- Manage stress effectively
- Periodization and variety

### Recovery Strategies

- Rest days and active recovery
- Quality sleep
- Stress management
- Adequate nutrition
- Hydration support
- Professional guidance if needed

## Working with Your Care Team

### When to Seek Help

- Persistent symptoms despite rest
- Significant performance decline
- Mood or motivation concerns
- Injury patterns
- Overall well-being concerns

## Important Considerations

- Individual thresholds vary
- Balance is key to sustainable activity
- Recovery is as important as training
- Professional guidance may be needed

## Important Note

This information is for health navigation and education only. It is not medical advice or a treatment plan. Overtraining concerns should be discussed with your care team.

## Next Steps

- Evaluate your current activity and recovery balance
- Notice signs of overtraining
- Adjust your approach as needed
- Discuss concerns with your care team
