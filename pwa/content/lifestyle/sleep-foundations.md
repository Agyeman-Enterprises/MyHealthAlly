# Sleep Foundations: Supporting Restorative Sleep

## Overview

Quality sleep is fundamental to health and well-being. This guide covers foundational principles for supporting better sleep.

## Why Sleep Matters

### Physical Health

- Recovery and repair
- Immune function
- Hormone regulation
- Energy restoration

### Mental and Emotional Health

- Cognitive function
- Emotional regulation
- Stress management
- Memory consolidation

## Sleep Hygiene Basics

### Consistent Schedule

- Regular bedtime and wake time
- Even on weekends
- Gradual adjustments if needed
- Supporting natural rhythms

### Sleep Environment

- Cool, dark, quiet room
- Comfortable bedding
- Minimize distractions
- Reserve bed for sleep

### Pre-Sleep Routine

- Wind-down activities
- Relaxing practices
- Limit screens before bed
- Create calming routine

## Daily Habits That Support Sleep

### Light Exposure

- Natural light during day
- Dim lights in evening
- Limit blue light before bed
- Support circadian rhythms

### Activity Patterns

- Regular physical activity
- Avoid intense exercise close to bedtime
- Movement throughout day
- Balance activity and rest

### Nutrition Considerations

- Regular meal timing
- Limit large meals before bed
- Be mindful of caffeine timing
- Consider alcohol's effects

## Common Sleep Challenges

### Difficulty Falling Asleep

- Relaxation techniques
- Consistent routine
- Address racing thoughts
- Consider environmental factors

### Waking During Night

- Return to sleep strategies
- Avoid clock watching
- Relaxation if needed
- Address underlying factors

### Feeling Unrested

- Evaluate sleep quality
- Consider sleep duration
- Review sleep environment
- Discuss with care team

## Important Considerations

- Individual sleep needs vary
- Sleep patterns change over time
- Health conditions can affect sleep
- Work with your care team for persistent issues

## Important Note

This information is for health navigation and education only. It is not medical advice or a treatment plan. For persistent sleep issues, discuss them with your care team, as they may indicate underlying health conditions.

## Next Steps

- Evaluate your current sleep patterns
- Implement gradual sleep hygiene improvements
- Track how changes affect your sleep
- Discuss persistent issues with your care team
