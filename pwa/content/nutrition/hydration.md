# Hydration: Supporting Your Body's Water Needs

## Overview

Adequate hydration is essential for health, but individual needs vary. Understanding hydration can help you meet your body's needs and work with your care team on health goals.

## Why Hydration Matters

### Essential Functions

- Temperature regulation
- Nutrient transport
- Waste removal
- Joint lubrication
- Digestive function
- Cognitive performance

### Signs of Adequate Hydration

- Regular urination
- Light-colored urine
- Good energy levels
- Clear thinking
- Healthy skin
- Overall well-being

## Individual Hydration Needs

### Factors That Influence Needs

- Activity level and intensity
- Climate and environment
- Body size and composition
- Health conditions
- Medications
- Life stage

### General Guidelines

- Most adults need 6-8 cups daily as a starting point
- Individual needs vary significantly
- Food and other beverages contribute
- Thirst is a helpful guide for many
- Context matters

## Sources of Hydration

### Water

- Plain water is an excellent choice
- Available and accessible
- No calories or additives
- Supports overall hydration

### Other Beverages

- Herbal teas
- Milk and milk alternatives
- Broths and soups
- Fruits and vegetables with high water content
- Other hydrating beverages

### Food Sources

- Many foods contain water
- Fruits and vegetables
- Soups and broths
- Contribute to overall hydration

## Signs of Dehydration

### Mild to Moderate

- Thirst
- Darker urine
- Reduced energy
- Dry mouth
- Headache

### More Severe

- Significant fatigue
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Very dark urine or reduced urination
- Confusion
- Requires immediate attention

## Important Considerations

- Individual needs vary significantly
- Context matters for hydration
- Balance is important
- Work with your care team for guidance

## Important Note

This information is for health navigation and education only. It is not medical advice or a treatment plan. Hydration needs should be discussed with your care team, especially if you have health conditions.

## Next Steps

- Evaluate your current hydration patterns
- Consider your individual needs
- Discuss hydration goals with your care team
- Work together on sustainable approaches
