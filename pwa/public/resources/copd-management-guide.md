## What is COPD?

**Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)** is a lung condition that makes it harder to breathe. It includes:
- **Chronic bronchitis** ‚Äî inflammation and mucus in airways
- **Emphysema** ‚Äî damage to air sacs in lungs

COPD develops slowly over years and is treatable. With proper management, you can live an active life.

## Daily Management

### ‚úÖ Green Zone ‚Äî Doing Well

**Symptoms:**
- Breathing feels normal for you
- Can do usual activities
- Sleeping well
- Normal mucus color

**Actions:**
- Take controller medications as prescribed
- Continue pulmonary rehab exercises
- Stay active within your limits
- Use oxygen as prescribed

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Yellow Zone ‚Äî Caution

**Symptoms:**
- More short of breath than usual
- Increased coughing
- More mucus or color change (yellow/green)
- Waking at night with breathing problems
- Needing rescue inhaler more often
- Fatigue or less energy

**Actions:**
1. Use rescue inhaler as directed
2. Increase rest periods
3. Practice pursed-lip breathing
4. **Call your care team** if not improving in 24-48 hours
5. Start action plan medications if prescribed

### üö® Red Zone ‚Äî Get Help Now

**Symptoms:**
- Severe shortness of breath at rest
- Can't speak in full sentences
- Lips or fingernails turning blue/gray
- Confusion or extreme drowsiness
- Chest pain
- Fever with chills
- Coughing up blood

**Actions:**
1. Use rescue inhaler
2. **Call 911** or go to ER immediately
3. Sit upright, stay calm
4. Have someone stay with you

## Your Medications

### Controller Medications (Take Daily)

These prevent symptoms ‚Äî **don't skip them even when feeling well**

| Type | Examples | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| **LABA** | Salmeterol, Formoterol | Long-acting bronchodilator |
| **LAMA** | Tiotropium, Umeclidinium | Opens airways 24 hours |
| **ICS** | Fluticasone, Budesonide | Reduces inflammation |
| **Triple Therapy** | Trelegy, Breztri | LABA + LAMA + ICS combined |

### Rescue Medications (Use When Needed)

| Type | Examples | When to Use |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **SABA** | Albuterol, Levalbuterol | Sudden shortness of breath |

**Tip:** If using rescue inhaler more than 2-3 times per week, tell your care team ‚Äî your controller may need adjustment.

## Oxygen Therapy

If you use supplemental oxygen:

### Key Rules

1. **Use as prescribed** ‚Äî don't adjust flow rate without asking
2. **Target 88-92% SpO2** ‚Äî higher is NOT better for COPD
3. **Never smoke near oxygen** ‚Äî fire hazard
4. **Travel planning** ‚Äî notify airline 48+ hours ahead
5. **Exercise** ‚Äî you may need higher flow during activity

### Common Flow Rates

| Activity | Typical Flow |
|----------|--------------|
| Rest | 1-2 L/min |
| Walking/Activity | 2-4 L/min |
| Sleep | May differ ‚Äî ask your provider |

## Preventing Flare-Ups (Exacerbations)

### Top Prevention Strategies

1. **Get vaccinated**
   - Flu shot yearly
   - Pneumonia vaccines
   - COVID-19 vaccines
   - RSV vaccine (if eligible)

2. **Avoid triggers**
   - Cigarette/vape smoke
   - Air pollution (check AQI)
   - Strong fumes and chemicals
   - Dust and allergens
   - Cold dry air (wear a scarf over mouth)

3. **Stay active**
   - Pulmonary rehabilitation
   - Daily walking (as tolerated)
   - Breathing exercises

4. **Maintain nutrition**
   - Eat smaller, frequent meals
   - Stay hydrated
   - Limit salt (reduces fluid retention)

5. **Manage stress**
   - Anxiety worsens breathlessness
   - Practice relaxation techniques
   - Join a support group

## Breathing Techniques

### Pursed-Lip Breathing

Use when short of breath:

1. Relax your shoulders
2. Breathe IN through your nose for 2 counts
3. Pucker lips like blowing out a candle
4. Breathe OUT slowly for 4 counts
5. Repeat until breathing eases

### Diaphragmatic Breathing

1. Sit comfortably or lie down
2. Place one hand on chest, one on belly
3. Breathe in ‚Äî belly should rise, chest stays still
4. Breathe out slowly ‚Äî belly falls
5. Practice 5-10 minutes daily
