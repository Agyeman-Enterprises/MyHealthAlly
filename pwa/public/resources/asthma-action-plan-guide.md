## Know Your Peak Flow

### Finding Your Personal Best

Your personal best is the highest number you can blow when feeling well.

**To find it:**
1. Record your peak flow 2-4 times daily for 2-3 weeks
2. Measure when asthma is well controlled
3. Your highest number = Personal Best

## Your Medicines Explained

### Controller Medicines (Take Every Day)

These PREVENT symptoms â€” take even when feeling well!

| Type | How It Works |
|------|--------------|
| **Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS)** | Reduces airway swelling |
| **Long-acting bronchodilators (LABA)** | Keeps airways open 12-24 hours |
| **Combination inhalers** | ICS + LABA together |
| **Leukotriene modifiers** | Blocks inflammation chemicals |
| **Biologics** | For severe asthma, reduces specific inflammation |

### Quick-Relief Medicines (Use When Needed)

These TREAT symptoms when they happen:

| Type | How It Works |
|------|--------------|
| **Short-acting bronchodilators (SABA)** | Opens airways fast (5-15 minutes) |
| **Examples:** Albuterol, Levalbuterol | Lasts 4-6 hours |

### Using Your Inhaler Correctly

**With a Spacer (recommended):**
1. Shake inhaler well
2. Attach to spacer
3. Breathe out completely
4. Put spacer mouthpiece in mouth
5. Press inhaler and breathe in SLOWLY
6. Hold breath 10 seconds
7. Wait 1 minute between puffs
