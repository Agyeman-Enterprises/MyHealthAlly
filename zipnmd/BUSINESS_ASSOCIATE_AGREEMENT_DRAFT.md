## 1. PURPOSE

MHA provides care coordination, data routing, and administrative support services involving Protected Health Information ("PHI") on behalf of Covered Entity.

**Services include:**
- Transmission of patient-submitted information
- Support for scheduling and follow-up
- Maintenance of secure records
- Facilitation of communication with care teams

---

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**Covered Entity:** The healthcare practice that is a covered entity under HIPAA.

**Business Associate:** MyHealthAlly, Inc., which performs functions or activities on behalf of Covered Entity involving PHI.

**Protected Health Information (PHI):** Individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form or medium.

**HIPAA:** Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, as amended.

**HITECH:** Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act.

## 4. SAFEGUARDS

### **4.1 Administrative Safeguards**

MHA shall:
- Designate a Privacy Officer
- Implement workforce training
- Establish access controls
- Maintain audit logs
- Conduct regular risk assessments

### **4.2 Physical Safeguards**

MHA shall:
- Secure facilities and workstations
- Control access to PHI
- Implement device and media controls
- Maintain environmental controls

### **4.3 Technical Safeguards**

MHA shall:
- **Encrypt PHI in transit** (TLS 1.2 or higher)
- **Encrypt PHI at rest** (AES-256 or equivalent)
- Implement access controls
- Implement audit controls
- Implement integrity controls
- Implement transmission security

### **4.4 Access Controls**

MHA shall:
- Limit access to authorized personnel only
- Implement role-based access controls
- Require unique user identification
- Implement automatic logoff
- Monitor access logs

---

## 5. REPORTING & BREACH NOTIFICATION

### **5.1 Unauthorized Use or Disclosure**

MHA shall:
- Report any unauthorized use or disclosure to Covered Entity
- Report without unreasonable delay (within 60 days as required by HIPAA)
- Provide details of the incident
- Cooperate in investigation

### **5.2 Breach Notification**

MHA shall:
- Notify Covered Entity of breaches without unreasonable delay
- Provide breach details:
  - Nature of breach
  - PHI involved
  - Individuals affected
  - Steps taken to mitigate
- Cooperate in mitigation efforts
- Assist in breach notification to individuals (if required)

---

## 6. SUBCONTRACTORS

### **6.1 Subcontractor Requirements**

MHA shall:
- Ensure any subcontractors comply with equivalent HIPAA obligations
- Execute written Business Associate Agreements with subcontractors
- Notify Covered Entity of subcontractors (upon request)
- Ensure subcontractors implement required safeguards

### **6.2 Subcontractor Breach**

If a subcontractor breaches PHI:
- MHA remains responsible to Covered Entity
- MHA shall notify Covered Entity
- MHA shall take corrective action

---

## 7. ACCESS, AMENDMENT, ACCOUNTING

### **7.1 Access Requests**

MHA shall:
- Assist Covered Entity in responding to patient access requests
- Provide PHI access as required by law
- Respond within timeframes required by HIPAA

### **7.2 Amendment Requests**

MHA shall:
- Assist Covered Entity in amending PHI
- Make amendments as directed by Covered Entity
- Document amendments in audit logs

### **7.3 Accounting of Disclosures**

MHA shall:
- Maintain accounting of disclosures
- Provide accounting to Covered Entity upon request
- Include required information per HIPAA

---

## 8. TERMINATION

### **8.1 Termination for Cause**

Either party may terminate this Agreement:
- Upon material breach
- If breach is not cured within 30 days of notice
- Immediately if breach cannot be cured

### **8.2 Effect of Termination**

Upon termination:
- **PHI shall be returned** to Covered Entity, or
- **PHI shall be securely destroyed** where feasible
- If destruction is not feasible, protections shall continue
- MHA shall not retain PHI except as required by law

### **8.3 Survival**

Provisions regarding:
- PHI return/destruction
- Breach notification
- Audit rights
- Indemnification

Shall survive termination.

---

## 9. NO ASSUMPTION OF CLINICAL RESPONSIBILITY

### **9.1 Clinical Decisions**

**Nothing in this Agreement assigns medical decision-making or clinical responsibility to MHA.**

MHA:
- Does not provide medical advice
- Does not make clinical decisions
- Does not diagnose or treat
- Does not replace healthcare providers

### **9.2 Covered Entity Responsibility**

Covered Entity:
- Makes all clinical decisions
- Provides all medical care
- Is responsible for patient outcomes
- Maintains clinical oversight

---

## 10. AUDIT RIGHTS

### **10.1 Covered Entity Rights**

Covered Entity may:
- Audit MHA's compliance with this Agreement
- Review security practices
- Review access logs
- Inspect facilities (with reasonable notice)

### **10.2 MHA Cooperation**

MHA shall:
- Cooperate with audits
- Provide requested documentation
- Allow reasonable inspection
- Maintain audit logs

- Medical malpractice
- Patient outcomes
- Use of PHI beyond scope of Agreement
